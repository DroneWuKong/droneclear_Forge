Phase II Predictions

Counter-UAS integration is the new filter. Phase II adds JIATF-401 actively trying to shoot down competitor drones. Systems without EW resilience or autonomous guidance will wash out. Fiber-optic drones (Skycutter/SkyFall) have inherent advantage — can't be jammed.
Price pressure eliminates low-volume manufacturers. Target drops from ~$5K to ~$3.5K per unit. Companies without scaled manufacturing or additive production (Firestorm xCell model) will struggle on margin.
New entrants allowed. DDP explicitly states "not a down-select process" — companies can join in Phase II that weren't in Phase I. AeroVironment, Redwire/Edge Autonomy, and other large primes may enter.
Ukrainian tech gets stronger. SkyFall (via Skycutter) won G1 with 99.3 points — 10+ points ahead of #2. Combat-proven Ukrainian tech iterating on 2-week cycles vs DOD's 6-month schedule. Senator Shaheen flagged this gap publicly.
Orqa/Firestorm partnership is the wildcard. Firestorm competed in G1 but didn't make top 11. The Orqa manufacturing partnership (280K drones/year capacity) plus Squall (NDAA-compliant FPV) plus xCell (expeditionary 3D printing) could change the equation for Phase II.
