How G1 Was Scored
Per Inside Unmanned Systems, Gauntlet I combined three equal-weighted categories: (1) Industrial capacity & readiness, (2) Mission performance, and (3) Warfighter ratings after 2 hours of operator training. A technically excellent drone could still score poorly on combined scoring if operators found it hard to use, or if the manufacturer couldn't scale. This is why Firestorm Labs (strong mission perf, strong funding) didn't make top 11, and why Napatree (highest warfighter ratings) did despite a mid-pack flight score.

Phase II Predictions

Based on verified Phase II test design per Inside Unmanned Systems. Phase II adds: comprehensive C-UAS applied to the operating environment, at least one night-operations mission, and emphasis on munition readiness including fully integrated training rounds.

Counter-UAS integration is the new filter (confirmed). Phase II officially applies "comprehensive C-UAS" to the operating environment. Systems without EW resilience or fiber-optic control will wash out. Skycutter's Shrike 10 Fiber (unjammable) and Neros+CX2's RF-hunter integration are the architectural winners here. ModalAI's VOXL 2 with GPS-denied VIO nav is the sleeper — autonomy survives when GPS and C2 both fail.
Night ops will expose teams that cut corners on EO/IR. Phase II requires at least one evaluation during night missions. Daylight-only sensors and missing IR illumination hardware will eliminate vendors that passed G1 on pure daytime flight perf. Nokturnal AI's name implies night-ops focus — this is their one strategic advantage.
Munition readiness is now explicit. Phase II adds "fully integrated training rounds" as a scoring dimension — meaning vendors must ship an OWA drone with a real warhead interface, not a bare airframe. Platform/software companies like Auterion may need closer OEM ties. Traditional munitions primes (Kratos SRE, possibly late entrants like AeroVironment) get a new door.
Price pressure eliminates low-volume manufacturers. Target drops from ~$5K to ~$3.5K per unit. Companies without scaled manufacturing (Neros at 2,500/mo, SkyFall at wartime scale) or additive production (Firestorm's xCell model) will struggle on margin.
New entrants allowed. DDG explicitly states "not a down-select process" — companies can join in Phase II that weren't in Phase I. AeroVironment, Redwire/Edge Autonomy, Anduril (via Switchblade-class), and other large primes may enter. Non-top-11 Phase I companies (Firestorm, Teal, PDW) also remain eligible.
Ukrainian tech gets stronger. Skycutter's Shrike 10 Fiber (UK-Ukraine collab via SkyFall) won G1 with 99.3 points — ~12 points ahead of #2 Neros. Combat-proven Ukrainian tech iterating on 2-week cycles vs DoD's 6-month schedule. Senator Shaheen flagged this gap publicly.
Firestorm/Orqa is the wildcard re-entry. Firestorm "ranked favorably for mission performance" per IUS but missed the combined leaderboard. If they pivot from the Group 2/3 Tempest 50 to the Orqa Squall FPV (NDAA-compliant, correct size class) and leverage xCell expeditionary 3D printing + Orqa's claimed 280K drones/yr capacity, they become a top-5 Phase II threat.
