I have a token. Now what?

Whether you subscribed or received a demo token, activation is the same. Takes about 30 seconds.

1
Go to Wingman AI
Navigate to /wingman/ — this is the main AI interface.
2
Open Settings
Tap the ⚙ gear icon in the top-right corner of the Wingman page. A settings panel will slide open.
3
Paste your token
Find the Subscription Token field near the top of the settings panel. Paste your full token — it starts with eyJ... and is several lines long. Copy the entire thing.
4
Hit Save & Connect
The panel will close, Claude will activate automatically, and you'll see the provider switch to Claude ✓. The token is stored locally — you won't need to re-enter it on this device.
5
Start using Pro features
Claude is now live in Wingman. The Gap Analyzer, PIE Predictions on /patterns/, and Compliance Reports on /report/ are all unlocked.
💡
Token stored per device. If you switch to a new phone or clear your browser data, you'll need to paste the token again. It's in the email we sent — search for "Wingman Pro access token" in your inbox.
⚠

// Wingman AI

How Wingman actually works

Wingman isn't just a chatbot. It's backed by the full Forge knowledge base — 3,639 components, 237 platforms, 49 handbook pages, 1,717 firmware targets, and 300+ intel articles — plus live web search on every query.

📡
When you ask a question, Wingman searches its local database first, pulls the most relevant context, then sends everything to the AI model. That's why it knows things like your FC's PID defaults, whether a specific ESC is NDAA compliant, or what "ELRS Airport mode" is without you explaining it.
→
Ask technical questions directly
No need to explain what Betaflight is, what MSP does, or what NDAA §848 means. Wingman already knows. Just ask: Is the DJI O3 Air Unit NDAA compliant? or What's the best NDAA-clean alternative to the Matrice 350?
→
Upload photos for visual triage
Tap the camera icon and attach a photo of your wiring, burnt ESC pads, or FC layout. Wingman will analyze the image and give you specific feedback. Works best with Claude (Pro) but also works with Gemini.
→
Use the quick-prompt chips
On first load, Wingman shows suggested prompts — tap any of them to get started fast. They're chosen to show what Wingman is actually good at.

// Gap Analyzer · Pro

How to use the Gap Analyzer

The Gap Analyzer compares two entities — platforms, components, vendors, or systems — across spec, compliance, and supply chain dimensions. It's in the Wingman sidebar.

1
Open Wingman AI and look for the Gap Analyzer panel
It's in the right sidebar on desktop, or scroll down on mobile. You'll see two input fields labeled Entity A and Entity B.
2
Enter two things to compare
Examples: Silvus SC3822 vs TrellisWare TW-950 · DJI Matrice 350 vs Skydio X10D · STM32H743 vs STM32H750
3
Hit Run Gap Analysis
Claude pulls context from the Forge database for both entities, then produces up to 8 gaps across spec (performance, RF, latency), compliance (NDAA, FCC, Blue UAS), and supply chain (Chinese OEM dependency, lead times, sole-source risk).
🔒
Pro required. The Gap Analyzer requires an active Pro subscription token in Settings. If you see a lock icon, paste your token first.
// PIE Intelligence · Pro

PIE Predictions and Gray Zone Scoring

PIE (Procurement Intelligence Engine) is a pipeline that runs daily, cross-validating supply chain signals, regulatory actions, and contract awards across three AI models. Go to /patterns/ to see the full dashboard.

◎
Free users see the gray zone flag list — which entities have flags and what type. Pro users unlock the full prediction cards with probability scores, confidence intervals, model breakdowns, and the gray zone risk scoring with sourced evidence.
→
Gray Zone flags — what they mean
A gray zone entity is a product or company that markets itself as domestic or independent but has material adversary connections — undisclosed DJI licensing, Chinese cloud infrastructure, adversary cryptographic keys, etc. Critical = don't procure. Warning = due diligence required.
→
Predictions — how to read probability scores
Each prediction shows probability (likelihood of event) and confidence (how much evidence supports the model). 80%+ probability with 80%+ confidence = act now. 60–79% = monitor closely. Below 60% = on the radar, not urgent.

// Gray Zone

What is a "gray zone" drone?

A gray zone product is one that appears domestic or independent but has material ties to adversary entities — typically undisclosed DJI technology, Chinese manufacturing, or data pipelines that route through Chinese cloud infrastructure.

⚠
This is not about being "made in China." Gray zone scoring is about undisclosed adversary technology dependency, data pipeline risk, and deceptive marketing claims. A product made in China with full transparency is a different risk profile than a product claiming US origin while running DJI firmware on DJI hardware.
→
Common gray zone signals
DJI SDK embedded · Firmware signed with DJI crypto keys · App connects to DJI cloud · PCB layout matches DJI products · No country of origin disclosed · Misleading "American-owned" claims · OTA updates through adversary infrastructure
→
Legal exposure for buyers
NDAA §848 prohibits procurement of covered entity products with federal funds. State and local agencies using federal grants (SOAR, DHS, etc.) are included. The Texas AG lawsuit against a gray zone vendor is the first signal that buyer liability is real — not just seller liability.
